## 1. How to File a Takedown Notice
If you are a copyright owner, authorized to act on behalf of one, or authorized to act under any exclusive right under copyright, please report alleged copyright infringements taking place on or through the App by completing a DMCA Notice of Alleged Infringement and delivering it to our Designated Copyright Agent.

Your notice must include the following six elements to be legally valid:

| Required Element | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Physical or Electronic Signature** | A signature of the copyright owner or a person authorized to act on their behalf. |
| **2. Identification of the Work** | A clear description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed (e.g., "My novel titled *The Lost Scribe*"). |
| **3. Identification of Infringing Material** | The exact location of the infringing material inside our App, including direct links, story IDs, or screenshots so we can locate it. |
| **4. Contact Information** | Your address, telephone number, and email address. |
| **5. Statement of Good Faith** | A statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law. |
| **6. Accuracy Statement** | A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the owner. |

Deliver the completed notice to our Designated Copyright Agent at:
* **Email:** copyright@[yourdomain].com
* **Attention:** DMCA Copyright Agent

## 2. Counter-Notifications
If you believe your story or cover artwork was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a Counter-Notification to our agent. This statement must include your signature, identification of the material that was removed, your contact info, and a statement consenting to the jurisdiction of the federal court system (or your local equivalent country court) to resolve the matter.
